Project Purpose
What is EventFlow?
EventFlow is an event-based application designed to bring people together on a social scale through shared interests. A user can log in, create an account, select interests, and meet people at events who share those passions. The app directly addresses how modern social media platforms are built to feed content rather than create real-world community and connection, with the high-level goal of creating a functional app that can eventually be developed on a bigger scale.
Core design priorities
Interest-based event matching
Over identity-based profile filtering — no swiping, no bias.
Low-pressure, no-scroll interface
Reduces cognitive load and performance anxiety for all users.
Small and large-scale events
Hosting for both individuals and businesses of any size.
Personalized event feed
Based on user-selected interests, location, and preferences.
Achievement system
Badges like Social Butterfly and Explorer gamify participation.
Business profiles
Companies can host and sponsor events with built-in analytics.

Research Essay
The research behind EventFlow

Most people can explain the discomfort of scrolling their addictive algorithmic platforms that feel like a stage performance and an information overload rather than a community. Being swarmed with advertisements and data closes the gap between online presence and lived experience. This problem has become the foundation for my research project. Researching this topic has helped me formulate the research question: How do existing social media platforms shape human interaction? Why do they fail to convert digital connections into real-world engagement, and how can event-based, interest-driven design better support meaningful community building? By examining existing scholarship and comparable digital tools, this paper outlines the conceptual foundations that shape the design of my prototype. The main goal of this capstone project is to answer the research question with extensive research on social media platforms and to create a functioning application that addresses the research question.
