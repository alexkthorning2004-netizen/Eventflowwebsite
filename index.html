The question driving this project

Project Purpose
What is EventFlow?
EventFlow is an event-based application designed to bring people together on a social scale through shared interests. A user can log in, create an account, select interests, and meet people at events who share those passions. The app directly addresses how modern social media platforms are built to feed content rather than create real-world community and connection, with the high-level goal of creating a functional app that can eventually be developed on a bigger scale.
Core design priorities
Interest-based event matching
Over identity-based profile filtering — no swiping, no bias.
Low-pressure, no-scroll interface
Reduces cognitive load and performance anxiety for all users.
Small and large-scale events
Hosting for both individuals and businesses of any size.
Personalized event feed
Based on user-selected interests, location, and preferences.
Achievement system
Badges like Social Butterfly and Explorer gamify participation.
Business profiles
Companies can host and sponsor events with built-in analytics.

Methods
This project followed a series of a multi-stage iterative design process, moving from hand-drawn sketches to a Figma prototype and finally a fully functional Python Streamlit application. Research methods included comparative platform analysis of existing apps like Meetup and Bumble BFF, alongside an academic literature review of 8 scholarly sources. Testing was conducted through a JSON-driven user simulation.

Full development journey in project log →
Key Findings / Process
What I found when doing research is that the answer isn't about user behaviour, it is about how a platform is designed. One of the biggest parts of my research was researching similar event based apps and how they succeed and how they fail. I realized that these platforms don't work because of how the algorithms are designed to keep people on the application with passive scrolling and identity based swiping and filtering. During the research I came across how these apps prioritize and work in a way that favours broad community structure rather than small interesting matched experiences. The thought process of creating the application's design can be explored in my research paper.
Challenges Faced
The original plan was to have the app tested and reviewed by peers and classmates to test the app's functionality and structure. Unfortunately, losing access to the ethics certification did not allow me to carry out this process.
Results Found
However, during my research I came up with an alternate idea to allow the apps functionality to be tested. I created a JSON file key that allowed me to create ten hypothetical users of the app that were able to log in, create accounts, host events and communicate with one another. This demonstrated the app's core purpose without active participants; the development journey can be seen in the project log.
Research Essay
The research behind EventFlow

Most people can explain the discomfort of scrolling their addictive algorithmic platforms that feel like a stage performance and an information overload rather than a community. Being swarmed with advertisements and data closes the gap between online presence and lived experience. This problem has become the foundation for my research project. Researching this topic has helped me formulate the research question: How do existing social media platforms shape human interaction? Why do they fail to convert digital connections into real-world engagement, and how can event-based, interest-driven design better support meaningful community building? By examining existing scholarship and comparable digital tools, this paper outlines the conceptual foundations that shape the design of my prototype. The main goal of this capstone project is to answer the research question with extensive research on social media platforms and to create a functioning application that addresses the research question.

Reflection
Looking back
What I Learned

When I came up with the idea of building an event-based application, I had the thought of creating a form of media that is different and unique from other social platforms. That is when I developed the research question, "How do existing social media platforms shape human interaction? Why do they fail to convert digital connections into real-world engagement, and how can event-based, interest-driven design better support meaningful community building?"

Skills Developed

Coming into this project I had no experience with building applications with Python or Streamlit. By the end of the project, I successfully built and deployed a full web application — see the code in the GitHub repository — with user authentication, event creation, messaging, notifications, achievements, business profiles, and a search system.

Future of the Project

The idea of Eventflow has the potential of becoming an actual platform. The next steps would include programming a full functional application that would be able to be released on application stores. Real life user testing would also be necessary to gather data and information from people's experience while using the app. From there, the app would benefit from a proper mobile build, real-time database integration to replace the JSON layer, and expanded features such as private event hosting and map-based event discovery. The business analytics screen is also worth developing further for commercial viability.
